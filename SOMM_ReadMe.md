## About the Project

SOMM is consulting for the Viticulture Commission of the Vinho Verde Region (CVRVV) in Portugal, the regulatory body responsible for certifying and promoting Vinho Verde wines from Northern Portugal. It controls, certifies, and promotes Vinho Verde wines, ensuring quality through laboratory analysis and granting the official seal of guarantee on bottles (Verde, n.d.). Our stakeholders are wondering what physicochemical qualities impact the quality of the wine and want to help wine companies produce high quality wine by understanding which properties affect quality.

## The Dataset

### Who collected it and how?
The data was collected by researchers Cortez et al. (2009) from the CVRVV for the purpose of predicting human wine taste preferences based on analytical tests at the certification step.

### Where was it sourced?
Sourced directly from UCI Machine Learning Repository

### What data is included?
6,497 Portuguese Vinho Verde wine samples (4,898 white, 1,599 red) with 11 physicochemical properties and quality ratings from human tasters on a 0–10 scale. The input variables are fixed acidity, volatile acidity, citric acid, residual sugar, chlorides, free sulfur dioxide, total sulfur dioxide, density, pH, sulphates, and alcohol, and the output variable is a quality rating on a 0–10 scale assigned by human tasters.


### Why was this dataset selected?
Group SOMM selected this dataset out of a shared passion for wine quality, stemming from study abroad experiences and a deepened appreciation for wine enjoyed across dinners and travels.

### Are there any ethical concerns?
Yes. There is no data exists on grape types, wine brand, or selling price. There are also far more normal wines than excellent or poor ones, and quality ratings are subjective, based on individual wine expert experience (Cortez et al., 2009).

## Stakeholders

### Who are the stakeholders?
- **Primary:** CVRVV board and vineyards/wine producers in the Vinho Verde region
- **Secondary:** Wine producers in other regions who may find the insights valuable

### What is their role?
CVRVV is the regulatory body that certifies and promotes Vinho Verde wines. Local wineries are the producers seeking to improve product quality.

### Why does this dataset matter to them?
It reflects the exact physicochemical properties measured at certification, making insights immediately actionable for producers.

### What are their goals?
To understand which physicochemical properties impact wine quality and use that knowledge to produce higher-quality wines.

---

## Context & Questions Being Answered

### What have stakeholders asked us to do?
Translate chemical wine data into a practical, easy-to-understand dashboard that CVRVV can share with local wineries.

### What problems are they trying to solve?
Wineries need to know which physicochemical properties drive quality ratings to make informed decision about production processes, and more easily predict taste preferences

### What specific questions do they want answered?
- What factors most influence Vinho Verde wine quality?
- How can we predict quality?
- Which properties do not affect quality?
- Do physicochemical properties affect white vs. red wine quality differently?
